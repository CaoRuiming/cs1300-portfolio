Iterative Design Writeup

In this project, I worked in a group with 3 other UI/UX students. We took a startup's concept and created desktop website designs for that startup using an iterative design process.

We chose to create a laptop/desktop-friendly website for Strive School, a startup educational institution that aims to be the Lambda School of Europe.

Here's a short description of Strive School from Y Combinator's Summer 2020 Demo Day 1:

Strive School is building a Lambda School for Europe, leveraging income-share agreements to train software engineers who don't pay for the education until they get a job in the industry. Once a graduate lands a role, the ISA terms charge the person 10% of salary for four years with a maximum total of €18,000.

With this company blurb in mind, we had the following main goals for our website:

  1. Communicate the unique business model and curriculum of Strive School to prospective students
  2. Allow interest prospective students to easily create Strive School accounts and begin the admissions process

While creating the prototype and high-fi mock up of the website, we deliberately avoided looking at the startup's existing website so that we independently make our own design.

Our target audience was prospective students who were interested in starting software development careers without having to commit to traditional 4-year degrees. Thus, we did not include interfaces for enrolled students in our prototypes.

Sketching and Wireframing

First, each group member made quick sketches of possible designs for the website. Each set of sketches included the following pages:

  • Home Page: includes company introduction, catchy stats, link to admissions page/process
  • Admissions Page: details admissions process, deadlines, includes link to sign up page/form
  • Curriculum Page: includes various coding languages being offered by the company

Initial sketches are below. Click on any one of them to see the images at full size.

After creating our initial sketches, we compared each other's ideas and consolidated them into a final wireframe that contained the layouts that we like most.

We chose the design elements in the pages seen below for the following reasons:

  • Home Page: In general, the goal we had in mind while designing this page was to get the user to apply to Strive School. Therefore, we made the "Apply Now" button very prominent: center on the screen and large in size. A person browsing the home page will likely only be on the page for a short while, so we put impressive statistics and prestigious companies on this page to succinctly provide flattering information to the user.
  • Admissions Page: A timeline in Admissions page helps prosepctive students have a clear understanding of the admission process and be more confident on the sequence of steps for applying to the program.
  • Curriculum Page: To give prospective students a broad overview of the technologies taught by courses in Strive School, we decided to list each topic out as cards grouped by curriculum track/focus. Clicking on each card would reveal more information about the respective technology (not implemented in the wireframe). We also wanted to emphasize that students could have choice in their Strive School learning experience.

Using our wireframe above, we got to work to create a hi-fi prototype of the webpage. We added additional pages to provide more information to the user, settled on a color scheme, and used varying font sizes and weights to draw the user's attention to certain aspects of the site.

Mockup Critique from Studio

Next, we presented our hi-fi prototype to other students and an industry critic in our studio for UIUX (CSCI 1300) by giving context for Strive School and walking through each page.

We collected the following points of feedback from our peers and industry critic.

  • 'Apply Now' form should be a popup instead of modal so that users don't have to leave the page that they're on
  • Make sure that 'Sign Up' page is GDPR compliant
  • Allow users with accounts to log in; provide some sort of link or button for this
  • Make the curriculum card modals scrollable
  • Add more information/context on what Strive School is
  • Add an About Us page
  • Emphasize on home page and throughout website what differentiates Strive School from other coding bootcamps

Revised Hi-fi Prototype

With the feedback from the Studio Critique in mind, we updated our hi-fi prototype with the following revisions:

  • Added a new 'Sign In' page, and renamed the Apply Now button in the navbar to 'Sign In'
  • Added an 'About Us' page to give the startup more credibility
  • Added a new section on the homepage to detail Strive School's unique business model
  • Added additional checkboxes to 'Sign Up' page to better comply with GDPR

User Testing

To get a final round of feedback on our website prototype, we set up and executed user tests for 3 testers through the website UserTesting.

Each test in UserTesting consists of a scenario, which gives context to testers before they interact with the prototype, and a series of tasks that the testers perform and give audio feedback on.

Testing Setup

Scenario:

This interactive website prototype (NOT a real website) was created for a coding bootcamp startup, Strive School. A coding bootcamp is an intensive program that prepares students for entry-level software development careers. We want to inform prospective students on why Strive School is unique and why students should apply. Imagine you are someone who is interested in starting a new career in software engineering without going through a traditional 4 year university. **This is not an actual website but an interactive mockup made on prototyping software.

  • Task 1: Without leaving the homepage, what are your initial impressions of the website? Explain your answer.
  • Task 2: Imagine you are interested in starting an application to Strive School. Find the admissions process and navigate to a page where you can create a Strive School account.
  • Task 3: Take as much time as you need to explore this website. Move on to the next task when you're ready.
  • Task 4: Go to Google and find one other company that offers a similar service. Compare the two websites. State at least 2-3 differences. Which did you prefer? Why?

Feedback from Testers

Task 1: Homepage Impressions

  • All three testers felt that the home page was plain and straightforward
  • Some testers requested that the home page be more exciting and eye-catching
  • It was not clear to one tester that there was more to the homepage than the top section with the image and "Apply Now" button
    • Requested smaller font
  • Overall positive impression
  • Analysis: Results were in line with our expectations, though we noted that it would likely be beneficial to include more information on the homepage.

Task 2: Finding Admissions Process and Sign Up Form

  • Very straightforward task; all 3 testers completed the task quickly and without issue
  • admissions page link and link to sign up pages were quickly found
  • Analysis: Results were in line with our expectations. We consider this part of the test to be a great success.

Task 3: Website Exploration

  • None of the 3 testers viewed all of the pages of the website
  • Curriculum page
    • Topic cards were not obviously clickable
    • Some testers wished for more details on courses up front
    • One tester thought that the fonts were boring and uniform across sections (requested more variety like bolding)
    • A few testers did not feel that the curriculum page was exciting
  • Some broken/confusing links threw off some testers
  • About Us page was generally liked
    • Gave more legitimacy to company
    • Testers liked the pictures of employees on the page
  • Analysis: We did discover a few things we might want to consider changing, but the results of this task were also largely in line with our expectations. Through the testers, we did discover a minor broken link ("Privacy Policy" being linked to the Terms and Conditions instead).

Task 4: Competitor Comparison

  • Competing websites had more details on the homepage
  • Other websites were flashier and more professional
  • Only one tester noted the unique business model on the homepage
  • Testers wanted our website to have more details on course timelines, dates, business model and tuition after graduation
  • Analysis: Results were within our expectations. We had expected to not compare favorably with competitors in terms of pure design, but we noted that our website was light on course and business model details.

Potential Interface Changes

  • More professional/exciting color palette and font choices
  • More information about school curriculum on the homepage
  • Responsive design to fit various laptop and desktop screens (website elements looked too big on smaller screens)
  • In response to user who did not realize that they could scroll on the homepage because the top section filled their screen
  • More animations and affordances to make clickable elements more clear (e.g. topic cards on the curriculum page)
  • An explicit homepage link in the navbar (users were confused about how to return to the homepage from other pages)
  • More details on timelines and dates on the admissions page and curriculum page
